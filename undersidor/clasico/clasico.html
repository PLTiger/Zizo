El Clásico

El Clásico, is a derby match in the sport of football, between the two poles of Spanish football, Real Madrid and FC Barcelona, ​​ and it is one of the forms of competition between the two largest and most important clubs in the Spanish capital Madrid and Barcelona, ​​the two largest cities in Spain. The word El Clasico is used for the matches that bring together the two teams in the Spanish League, but nowadays, any match that brings them together is known as El Clásico, whether the tournament is the King's Cup, the European Champions League, or anything else. This match represents a struggle between the two largest cities in Spain, and is sometimes referred to as a political conflict between the two cities, between the capital Madrid, which is seen as the bearer of Spanish nationalism, and Barcelona, ​​which carries the banner of Catalan nationalism.



It is the most watched match in the world, with the number of viewers reaching hundreds of millions. In the match that took place in April 2017, the number of viewers reached 650 million. This derby is not only considered one of the most famous European derbies, but also one of the most famous in the world, as it represents a struggle between two teams who collectively won 18 European Champions League and 60 domestic league championships.


The two clubs are among the richest and most successful football clubs in the world. Real Madrid is the largest club in terms of fan base, according to a 2007 Harvard University study. Also in 2007 by the Spanish Social Research Center, a survey showed that Real Madrid is supported by 32% of the Spanish population, while 25% expressed their support for Barcelona. According to a poll conducted by the Ikerfel Foundation, in 2011 and published in the newspaper AS, the poll showed that Barcelona are the most popular team in Spain with 44%, while Real Madrid are in second place with 37%. A poll conducted by the German sports research agency + Markt in 2010 revealed that Barcelona is more popular in Europe than Real Madrid, as Barcelona has nearly 57.8 million fans across Europe, while Real Madrid has only 31.3 million fans. The fans of both clubs are at the forefront in terms of follow-up and admiration on the official pages of the two clubs on social media sites like Facebook, Twitter and others compared to other clubs, with 117 million followers and admirers for Barcelona club official pages and 108 million for Real Madrid official pages, according to a statistic published by the Spanish newspaper Marca.
